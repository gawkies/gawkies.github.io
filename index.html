How MLB Drafts Are Picked.

In June every year, the MLB drafts are held where the 30 MLB clubs come together via conference call. All 30 clubs choose their picks in reverse order based on their previous season's standings. The rules for eligibility are that:

  • 1. You must be a resident or have attended an educational institute inside the United States, Canada, Or a U.S Territory. Players from other regions are not subject to the draft and can be signed by any team unless they have been to an educational institute in the U.S.
  • 2. Have never signed a major or minor league contract.
  • 3. High school players are not eligible untill they have graduated.
  • 4. Players at four-year universities or colleges are eligible three years after enrollment, or after their 21st birthday. (Whichever comes first.)
  • 5. Junior and community college players are elligible to be drafted at any point in time.
